Research progress

Segmentation of spruce budworm

Three experiments select the model, the input channels, the target, and the loss.

Methods and data

Each radar scene becomes a set of input channels and a binary target. A model predicts the plume mask. Training uses 256 pixel patches. Evaluation uses full scenes on the held out test set. The split is fixed and shared across all experiments, so the results are comparable.

Data

Radar
XAM, Val d'Irene, Quebec. 960 by 960 grid, 500 m pixels.
Scenes
1579 positive, 1500 negative. Years 2013 to 2019, July and August.
Split
Stratified by year 70 / 20 / 10. Test 170 positive scenes.

Methods compared

ArchitecturesUNet, UNet++, Attention UNet, nnUNet, DeepLabV3+, SegFormer
LossesDice + BCE, Focal, Tversky, Focal Tversky, Dice + Boundary
Targetsany echo, dBZ ≥ 0, dBZ ≥ 5
Channelsradar reflectivity, DEM, beam height, valid mask

Metrics

MetricDefinitionFormula

TP, FP, FN are the counts of true positive, false positive, and false negative pixels. P and G are the predicted and truth masks, ∂P and ∂G their boundaries, and τ a tolerance in pixels. Σ sums over all pixels. Dice, IoU, precision, recall, Boundary IoU, NSD, HD95, and ASSD are averaged over the positive test scenes. Background FP is measured on the negative test scenes.
